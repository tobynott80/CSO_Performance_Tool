Test 1 - Dry Day Discharges


What is a Dry Day Discharge?



  • Any discharge that occurs or continues on a “dry day”, allowing “one dry day” after rainfall ends. Where a “dry day is a calendar day with less than 0.25mm total rainfall depth”


    • How does the application identify Dry Day Discharges?


  • Divide the year into calendar days and sum the total rainfall depth for each day
  • Classify them as ‘Wet Day’, ‘Previous Day Wet’, ‘Dry Day’
  • Export the spill stats report from the model run to analyse. If any part of the spill event is during a day classified as ‘Dry Day’, that spill is a dry day discharge and the CSO is unsatisfactory.
