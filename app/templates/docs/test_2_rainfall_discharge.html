Test 2 - Moderate Rainfall Discharges


What are Moderate Rainfall Discharges?


  • Where a rainfall event totalling 4 mm or more in any one-hour period has been recorded at a representative raingauge in the previous 24hrs, then the spill can be considered as “due to heavy rainfall”.

  • Guidance ambiguity: - Rainfall depth in 1 hour vs. Rainfall intensity of 4mm/h at any point - 24hrs preceding the start of the spill only or the entire spill duration needs to comply


    • How does the application identify Moderate Rainfall Discharges?


  • From stats report, look at the start of spill and retrieve rainfall data for the previous 24 hrs.
  • Perform a rolling 1hr sum on the rainfall data selected.
  • If any of the sums is greater than 4mm the spill is “due to heavy rainfall”. If not. it is a moderate rainfall spill and the CSO is substandard
